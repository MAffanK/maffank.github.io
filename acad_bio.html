Active Flexible Compliant-Based Bionic Arm

A biomedical device project developed under Prof. S. Kanagaraj at IIT Guwahati, focused on designing a lightweight, compliant, and 3D-printed bionic arm for affordable and adaptive prosthetic solutions.

Overview

The project presents the design and development of an actively controlled, flexible bionic arm that leverages compliant mechanisms and additive manufacturing to reduce mechanical complexity and enhance user comfort. The device is cost-effective, customizable, and mimics natural human motion without the use of traditional rigid joints.

3D model of compliant gripper mechanism

Core Concept

The arm is based on a compliant mechanism topology that transfers motion through elastic deformation rather than discrete moving parts. Its actuation is achieved by:

  • Reading muscle sensor signals to drive servo motors via an ESP32 microcontroller.
  • Using 3D-printed PLA structures for lightweight, biocompatible design.
  • Employing PD control to reduce latency and improve motion precision.

Servo assembly and muscle sensor integration

Implementation & Analysis

The design was iteratively analyzed in ANSYS Mechanical to evaluate stress and deformation under real operating conditions. Once optimized, all components were 3D printed and assembled to produce a functional prototype capable of responsive actuation.

Stress and deformation contours of the compliant arm

Achievements & Learnings

  • Developed a fully functional active bionic arm prototype.
  • Reduced response lag and improved accuracy using PD-based actuation control.
  • Validated compliant mechanism design for complex motion paths through both simulation and testing.
  • Gained hands-on experience in topology design, control programming, and 3D printing.

Active compliant arm in operation

Future Scope

  • Integrate tactile feedback sensors using strain-gauge technology.
  • Expand to multi-DOF actuation for enhanced flexibility.
  • Experiment with Nitinol wire actuators for muscle-like movement and improved bio-mimicry.
