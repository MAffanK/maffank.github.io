Active Flexible Compliant-Based Bionic Arm

Compliant MechanismESP32PD Control 3D PrintingANSYS

A biomedical-device project under Prof. S. Kanagaraj at IIT Guwahati — a lightweight, compliant, 3D-printed bionic arm for affordable and adaptive prosthetics.

Overview

The project develops an actively controlled, flexible bionic arm that uses compliant mechanisms and additive manufacturing to cut mechanical complexity and improve comfort. It is cost-effective, customisable, and mimics natural motion without traditional rigid joints.

Gripper model coming soon

Core concept

The arm uses a compliant-mechanism topology that transfers motion through elastic deformation rather than discrete moving parts. Actuation works by:

  • Reading muscle-sensor signals to drive servo motors via an ESP32.
  • Using 3D-printed PLA structures for a lightweight, biocompatible design.
  • Applying PD control to reduce latency and improve motion precision.
Servo & sensor setup coming soon

Implementation & analysis

The design was iteratively analysed in ANSYS Mechanical for stress and deformation under real operating conditions. Once optimised, all components were 3D printed and assembled into a functional prototype with responsive actuation.

Achievements

  • Built a fully functional active bionic-arm prototype.
  • Reduced response lag and improved accuracy via PD-based actuation.
  • Validated the compliant design for complex motion paths in simulation and testing.
  • Hands-on experience in topology design, control programming and 3D printing.

Future scope

  • Integrate tactile feedback using strain-gauge sensors.
  • Expand to multi-DOF actuation for greater flexibility.
  • Explore Nitinol-wire actuators for muscle-like motion and better bio-mimicry.
