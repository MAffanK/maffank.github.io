Generative AI for Structural & Thermal–Fluid Topology Optimisation

Deep LearningGANs / CNNsTopology Optimisation Darcy Thermal–Fluid SolverPython · TensorFlow

My M.Tech research (under Prof. D. Sharma, IIT Guwahati): a generative-design framework that learns to produce optimised material layouts directly — replacing slow iterative solvers with deep-learning surrogates.

The problem

Topology optimisation finds the best way to distribute material in a domain for a given load or flow — but classical methods are iterative and expensive, re-solving a full physics problem every step. The goal of this work is to learn that mapping once, then generate optimised designs in a single forward pass.

Optimised layouts — coming soon

Approach

I generate training data from a 2D Darcy-based thermal–fluid solver and structural optimisation runs, then train deep-learning surrogates (CNN/GAN-style generative models) to predict optimal topologies from the boundary conditions and loads.

The surrogate reaches an R² of 0.9998 on structural topology-optimised datasets — effectively reproducing the optimiser's output, near-instantly.

Highlights

  • R² 0.9998 surrogate accuracy on generated structural topology datasets.
  • Custom 2D Darcy thermal–fluid solver for generating thermal-flow optimisation data.
  • Generative pipeline that outputs optimised designs in a single pass, instead of hundreds of solver iterations.
  • Phase 2 focus: end-to-end thermal–fluid topology optimisation with DL surrogates.
