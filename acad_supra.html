Supra SAE India

B.Tech Major Project | Aug 2018 – Jul 2019

Design & Manufacturing of Powertrain Components

As part of the Supra SAE India 2019 competition, our team designed and built a formula-style race car adhering to SAE standards. My focus was on the powertrain system — specifically designing, analyzing, and fabricating components such as engine mounts and transmission sprockets.

I also developed detailed CAD models for over 40 components across subsystems, integrating them into the full vehicle assembly using SolidWorks. These designs emphasized weight reduction, manufacturability, and vibration stability.

Vehicle Integration & Structural Analysis

The powertrain components were integrated into the vehicle chassis designed using tube-frame geometry. FEA and dynamic simulations were performed to ensure that high-stress regions were reinforced while keeping the overall mass optimized for performance.

The final design met safety and performance criteria, ready for manufacturing and on-track evaluation.

Cost Analysis & Competition Performance

I prepared a detailed cost analysis report covering materials, machining, and manufacturing feasibility. Each part was benchmarked for cost efficiency and compliance with SAE India’s budget criteria.

The vehicle was successfully presented and competed at the national event, demonstrating robust design, precision manufacturing, and collaborative teamwork.
