CFD Analysis of a Turbine Blade

An M.Tech project focusing on fluid dynamics and simulation.

Project Overview

This project involved a comprehensive Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) analysis of a new turbine blade design aimed at improving aerodynamic efficiency. The primary objective was to simulate airflow under various operational conditions to identify areas of turbulence and pressure loss. Using ANSYS Fluent, a 3D model was meshed and subjected to rigorous testing to validate its performance against theoretical calculations.

Methodology & Tools

The blade geometry was first modeled in SolidWorks and then imported into ANSYS for meshing and simulation. The k-epsilon turbulence model was employed to accurately capture the fluid behavior. Post-processing was done using CFD-Post to visualize velocity vectors, pressure contours, and streamline plots, which were crucial for the final analysis and design recommendations.

Key Findings

  • Achieved a 7% reduction in drag compared to the baseline design.
  • Identified a critical angle of attack for optimal performance.
  • Validated simulation results with wind tunnel experimental data.
