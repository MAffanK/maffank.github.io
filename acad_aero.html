SAE ISS Aero Design Challenge

A student-built airplane project developed for the SAE ISS Aero Design Challenge. The plane was designed for a 2 m wingspan category with a 5 kg payload target, later extended in my BTech work to include solar power instrumentation for greatly increased endurance.

Project Summary

The team designed, manufactured and flight-tested a 2 m wingspan UAV capable of carrying a 5 kg payload. Primary competition goals were structural efficiency, low drag, and robust flight performance. In a BTech continuation I proposed integrating solar panels on the upper wing to extend mission endurance — theoretical calculations indicated a sunrise-to-sunset range on the order of ≈ 550 km under ideal conditions.

My Role

I was responsible for the fuselage design and the CFD analysis of the wing and full aircraft. I created the complete CAD model in SolidWorks, performed aerodynamic studies (drag/polar curves), and validated designs through ANSYS (including composite checks with ANSYS ACP). For the BTech continuation I designed the solar power system and carried out energy & range calculations.

Tools & Analysis

The project combined CAD, CFD, composite analysis and data-driven performance estimation:

  • SolidWorks — full aircraft CAD and manufacturing drawings.
  • ANSYS (CFX/Fluent & ACP) — aerodynamic CFD runs, flow visualization and composite layup simulation.
  • MATLAB — plotting, drag vs velocity curves, propeller thrust vs velocity, solar energy calculations and range predictions.
